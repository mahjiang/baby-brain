# Newborn Care Basics

> What to know for the first days and weeks. Fill in details from classes and provider guidance.

## The First Days

### Normal Newborn Things (Don't Panic)
- Irregular breathing patterns during sleep
- Hiccups (very common)
- Sneezing (not necessarily a cold — clearing nasal passages)
- Peeling skin
- Acne (baby acne is normal at 2-4 weeks)
- Jaundice (mild is common; provider monitors)
- Umbilical cord stump falls off in 1-3 weeks (keep clean and dry)
- Soft spots (fontanelles) — normal, handle gently

### When to Call the Pediatrician
- Fever over 100.4°F (38°C) rectally in a baby under 3 months — **this is an emergency**
- Not feeding well or refusing to eat
- Fewer than expected wet/dirty diapers
- Difficulty breathing
- Persistent vomiting (not just spit-up)
- Unusual lethargy or difficulty waking
- Yellow skin getting worse (jaundice)

## Daily Care

### Feeding
- Newborns eat 8-12+ times per day (every 2-3 hours)
- Track feeds and diapers in first weeks
- See [feeding section](../06-feeding/) for details

### Diapering
- Expect 8-12 diaper changes per day
- Wet diapers: at least 6/day by day 4
- Meconium (first poops) is dark/tarry — this is normal
- See [diapering](../02-gear/diapering.md)

### Sleep
- Newborns sleep 14-17 hours/day in short bursts
- Always on back, on firm flat surface, nothing in crib
- See [sleep](sleep.md) for safe sleep guidelines

### Bathing
- Sponge baths until cord stump falls off
- Full baths 2-3 times per week (not daily — dries skin)
- Lukewarm water, fragrance-free soap

### Cord Care
- Keep clean and dry
- Fold diaper below stump
- Falls off in 1-3 weeks
- Call pediatrician if: red, swollen, smells bad, or oozing pus
