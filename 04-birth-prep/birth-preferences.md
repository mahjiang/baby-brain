# Birth Preferences

> **Status:** Researching | Discuss with provider by ~week 32-34

This is a starting framework, not a rigid plan. Birth is unpredictable. The goal is to communicate preferences while staying flexible.

## How to Use This Document

1. Research and fill in preferences below
2. Discuss with partner
3. Review with OB/midwife at a prenatal visit (~week 32-34)
4. Print a short, 1-page version for the hospital bag
5. Share with anyone on the birth team (doula, support person)

## Key Considerations for Our Situation

- Pregnancy complications: discuss with provider how these may affect birth plan options
- See [complications](../01-pregnancy/complications.md) for details
- Some preferences below may need to be adjusted based on medical needs

## Delivery Preferences

### During Delivery
- [ ] Partner to announce sex (if not finding out beforehand) / already know
- [ ] Mirror to see delivery: yes / no / maybe
- [ ] Who cuts the cord: partner / provider / no preference
- [ ] Delayed cord clamping (1-3 minutes — evidence supports benefits; AAP recommends)
  - *Evidence: Delayed clamping increases iron stores and hemoglobin levels in newborns (Source: [AAP, ACOG](https://publications.aap.org/pediatrics/article/139/6/e20170957/38635))*

### Immediately After Birth
- [ ] Skin-to-skin immediately (if baby is stable)
- [ ] Delay routine procedures (weighing, bathing) for first hour of skin-to-skin
- [ ] Breastfeeding initiation within first hour (if planning to breastfeed)
- [ ] Vitamin K injection: yes (AAP strongly recommends)
  - *Evidence: Prevents Vitamin K deficiency bleeding, a rare but serious condition (Source: [AAP](https://publications.aap.org/pediatrics/article/149/3/e2021056036/184962))*
- [ ] Erythromycin eye ointment: yes (standard, required by law in most states)
- [ ] Hepatitis B vaccine: yes (AAP recommends within 24 hours)
