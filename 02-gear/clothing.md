# Baby Clothing Guide

> **Used OK:** YES — clothes are perfect for secondhand, hand-me-downs, and Buy Nothing groups.

## What You Actually Need (Newborn–3 Months)

| Item | Quantity | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Zip-up sleepers / footie pajamas | 5-7 | These are the MVP. Day and night outfit. Zips > snaps. |
| Short-sleeve onesies / bodysuits | 5-7 | Layer under sleepers or standalone in warm weather |
| Going-home outfit | 1 | Something cute with easy access (zip or loose) |
| Socks / booties | 3-4 pairs | They fall off constantly. Don't invest heavily. |
| Hat (for sun/warmth) | 1-2 | Newborns lose heat through their heads |
| Scratch mittens | 2 pairs | Or just use sleepers with fold-over cuffs |
| Light jacket / bunting (seasonal) | 1 | If baby arrives in cooler weather |

## Sizing Guide

| Size | Typical Age | Weight |
|------|-------------|--------|
| Preemie | Premature / very small | Up to ~6 lbs |
| Newborn (NB) | 0-4 weeks | Up to ~8 lbs |
| 0-3 months | 0-12 weeks | 8-12 lbs |
| 3-6 months | 3-6 months | 12-16 lbs |

**Tip:** Many full-term babies skip newborn size entirely. Buy more 0-3 month than NB. Due in July = warm weather baby, so lean toward lighter clothes.

### Brand Sizing Notes

*Added 2026-02-28*

Not all baby clothing brands size the same. Here is a rough guide:

| Brand | Sizing Tendency | Notes |
|-------|----------------|-------|
| **Carter's** | Runs slightly small / long & lean fit | Most popular US brand; size up if in doubt. Thicker, softer fabric. |
| **Gerber** | Runs small (micro-petite fit) | Size up 1-2 sizes vs Carter's. A baby in Carter's 3M may need Gerber 6M. Thinner fabric, shrinks after first wash. |
| **Cat & Jack (Target)** | True to size / slightly generous | Good everyday basics. |
| **H&M** | Runs large (Euro sizing) | Their 2-4M often fits like US 3-6M. |
| **Primary** | True to size | Higher quality basics, no logos/graphics. Pricier. |

**Bottom line:** Don't stock up heavily on one size from one brand. Mix brands and sizes, especially before baby arrives and you know their actual build. (Source: [Baby Clothes Battle: Brand Comparison](https://bumptobrilliance.com/2025/01/19/baby-clothes-battle-my-experience-with-top-brands/))

## Pro Tips

- Zip-up > snaps (especially at 3 AM)
- Stretchy cotton is easiest to get on/off
- Skip jeans, shoes, and "outfits" for newborns — function over fashion
- Accept all hand-me-downs; donate what you don't use
- Organize by current size + next size in separate bins

## Summer Baby Tips

*Added 2026-02-28*

July due date means baby's first months are peak summer. Here is what matters.

### Dressing for Heat

- **One layer rule:** In warm weather, dress baby in a single layer of lightweight, breathable fabric. A short-sleeve cotton onesie or a loose-fitting romper is plenty. (Source: [Children's Health — Keeping Baby Safe in Summer](https://www.childrens.com/health-wellness/keeping-your-baby-safe-and-cool-in-summer))
- **Fabric matters:** Stick with cotton. It breathes better than polyester, nylon, or synthetic blends. Light colors reflect heat.
- **Overheating is riskier than being slightly cool.** Signs of overheating: flushed skin, rapid breathing, fussiness, damp hair. When in doubt, remove a layer.
- **Skip the hat indoors.** Hats outdoors for sun protection, but indoors they can trap heat.

### Dressing for AC / Indoors

- Most buildings and homes run AC at 68-72 degrees F in summer. At that range, a short-sleeve onesie with a light swaddle or sleep sack is fine for sleep. (Source: [AAP Safe Sleep Recommendations via HealthyChildren.org](https://www.healthychildren.org/English/ages-stages/baby/sleep/Pages/default.aspx))
- For naps in air-conditioned rooms, add one layer over what you would comfortably wear.
- **Room temperature sweet spot for sleep: 68-72 degrees F.** Use a room thermometer rather than guessing.

### Sun Protection

- **AAP recommends keeping babies under 6 months out of direct sunlight.** Their skin is too sensitive and they cannot regulate temperature well. (Source: [AAP Sun Safety — HealthyChildren.org](https://www.healthychildren.org/English/safety-prevention/at-play/Pages/Sun-Safety.aspx))
- When outdoors: use a wide-brim hat, lightweight long sleeves and pants, and stroller shade covers.
- **Sunscreen under 6 months:** Only as a last resort on small exposed areas (face, hands) if shade/clothing is not available. Use mineral sunscreen (zinc oxide or titanium dioxide). (Source: [FDA — Should You Put Sunscreen on Infants?](https://www.fda.gov/consumers/consumer-updates/should-you-put-sunscreen-infants-not-usually))
- **Avoid being outside for extended periods when the heat index exceeds 90 degrees F.** Newborns do not sweat effectively and are prone to heat-related illness. (Source: [AAP via Children's Health](https://www.childrens.com/health-wellness/keeping-your-baby-safe-and-cool-in-summer))
- Peak sun hours (10 AM - 2 PM) are highest risk — plan outings for mornings or evenings.

### Car Seat Clothing Safety

This applies year-round but is especially relevant for transitional seasons (late summer into fall):

- **No bulky clothing under car seat harness straps.** Puffy jackets, bunting, and thick layers compress in a crash, creating slack in the harness. Baby can be ejected from the seat. (Source: [AAP Winter Car Seat Safety Tips — HealthyChildren.org](https://www.healthychildren.org/English/safety-prevention/on-the-go/Pages/Winter-Car-Seat-Safety-Tips.aspx))
- **Safe approach:** Strap baby in wearing thin layers only. Place a blanket or coat over the buckled harness for warmth.
- **Summer version of this problem:** Avoid leaving baby in a hot car seat — the buckle and straps can burn skin. Cover the car seat with a light towel when parked. Check buckle temperature before strapping baby in.
- A thin cotton onesie or bodysuit is ideal car seat clothing in summer.

### What to Actually Buy for a July Baby

| Item | Priority | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Short-sleeve onesies / bodysuits | High | Your workhorse. Buy more of these than sleepers. |
| Lightweight rompers | Medium | One-piece outfits that are easy and breezy. |
| Thin cotton sleepers (footless OK) | Medium | For AC nights. Footless keeps them cooler. |
| Wide-brim sun hat (UPF 50+) | High | Essential for any outdoor time. |
| Lightweight muslin swaddles | High | Double duty: swaddle, shade cover, nursing cover, light blanket. |
| Light cardigan or zip hoodie | Low | For overly air-conditioned spaces. One is enough. |

> **Summer baby shortcut:** You need fewer clothes overall because laundry is easier (small items dry fast) and baby is mostly in a diaper + onesie at home. Don't overbuy.
