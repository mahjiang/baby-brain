# Stroller

> **Status:** Decided | **Buy by:** Week 30-32

## Our Requirements

- **Car seat compatible** — Must work with chosen infant seat (see [car seat](car-seat.md))
- **Transit friendly** — Easy to fold one-handed, compact fold, lightweight enough to carry up/down stairs
- **Car friendly** — Fits in our trunk
- **Apartment friendly** — Compact storage footprint
- **Handles city terrain** — Sidewalks, curbs, possible rough pavement

## Types to Consider

| Type | Best For | Pros | Cons |
|------|----------|------|------|
| **Travel system** | Simplicity | Car seat + stroller matched; one purchase | Often bulky, heavy |
| **Full-size + adapter** | Longevity | Best ride, features, lasts years | Heavy, expensive |
| **Lightweight / umbrella** | Transit | Light, compact fold | Less sturdy, fewer features |
| **Compact / city stroller** | Apartment + transit | Small fold, light, still full-featured | Pricier for quality ones |

> **Bottom line for us:** Compact / city stroller is the strongest category match. We need light weight for transit stairs, small fold for the apartment, and car seat compatibility for the infant stage. A compact stroller with a car seat adapter gives us the best of a travel system without the bulk.

## Research & Reviews

*Added 2026-02-28*

### Overview: Compact City Strollers

The compact/city stroller category has matured significantly. All five models above fold small enough for airline overhead bins (IATA-compliant), which also means they store well in apartments and car trunks. The main trade-offs are between weight, fold compactness, ride quality, and features like basket size and canopy coverage. (Source: [Fathercraft Travel Stroller Tests](https://fathercraft.com/best-travel-strollers/))

### Model-by-Model Analysis

#### Cybex Libelle 2 — Best Budget Compact

- **Weight:** 13.7 lbs — among the lightest options
- **Fold:** Ultra-compact (12.6" x 7.9" x 18.9"), the smallest folded footprint of any stroller in this class. Folds like an umbrella into a slim profile. Does NOT stand when folded
- **Seat:** Reclines but does not lie flat; seat usable from 6 months (needs car seat for younger). Max child weight 55 lbs
- **Canopy:** Extra-large UPF 50+ sun canopy — surprisingly good for the price
- **Basket:** Adequate but not spacious
- **Terrain:** Handles smooth pavement fine; struggles on rougher surfaces
- **Car seat compatibility:** Cybex Cloud/Aton series, Nuna PIPA, Maxi-Cosi Mico Luxe. Adapter included with Cybex seats; separate adapter for others
- **One-hand fold:** Yes, but two-step process
- **Price:** ~$300 — significantly cheaper than competitors
- **Best for:** Budget-conscious buyers who prioritize light weight and tiny fold over ride quality
- **Weakness:** No standing fold, limited recline (not from birth without car seat), less sturdy ride on rough terrain

(Source: [Cybex Official](https://www.cybex-online.com/en/us/libelle-gold-stroller.html), [Baby Gizmo Review](https://babygizmo.com/cybex-libelle-stroller-review-the-ultimate-compact-travel-companion-for-families/))

#### Babyzen YOYO2 / Stokke YOYO3 — Lightest Full-Featured

- **Weight:** 13.6 lbs (frame: 11.2 lbs) — tied for lightest in class
- **Fold:** Flat fold (20" x 17" x 7"), very thin profile. Includes shoulder strap for carrying. Does NOT stand when folded, but the flat shape slides into closets and under beds easily
- **Seat:** Multi-position recline. 6+ seat for 6 months+; optional 0+ Newborn Pack ($120+) or Bassinet ($300+) for birth use. Max 48.5 lbs
- **Canopy:** Adequate but small peekaboo window; not the most coverage in class
- **Basket:** Small (YOYO2: 10 lb limit, YOYO3: 22 lb limit). Access is blocked by frame bars — hard to get a large diaper bag in
- **Terrain:** All-wheel independent suspension (Hytrel elastomer on YOYO3). Handles city sidewalks, parks, and moderate rough surfaces well. *Mixed reviews on cobblestones*
- **Car seat compatibility:** Clek Liing, Cybex Aton/Cloud, Maxi-Cosi Pebble/Mico, Nuna PIPA series — all via adapter ($40-50 sold separately). Does NOT work with Graco seats
- **One-hand fold:** *Inconsistent reviews.* Some reviewers find the fold mechanism fiddly and difficult one-handed. Two latches to release (Source: [Fathercraft](https://fathercraft.com/best-travel-strollers/))
- **Price:** YOYO2 ~$449; Stokke YOYO3 (2024 successor with improved basket, taller seat back, better suspension) ~$499
- **Note on YOYO2 vs YOYO3:** Stokke acquired Babyzen; the YOYO3 is the direct successor. Key upgrades: basket capacity doubled (10 lbs to 22 lbs), taller seat back (+2"), improved suspension, reflective wheels, perforated seat for breathability. Same frame weight and fold size. All YOYO2 accessories are compatible. If buying new, get the YOYO3 — the basket upgrade alone is worth it
- **Best for:** Lightest carry weight for subway stairs; flat fold is great for tight storage
- **Weakness:** Basket access and size (even on YOYO3), fold mechanism complaints, small canopy

(Source: [Strolleria YOYO3 vs YOYO2 Comparison](https://strolleria.com/blogs/news/stokke-yoyo-vs-babyzen-yoyo2-stroller-comparison), [Stokke Official](https://www.stokke.com/USA/en-us/strollers/yoyo-strollers))

#### Nuna TRVL — Best Self-Fold Mechanism

- **Weight:** 13.6 lbs (without canopy and arm bar) — tied for lightest
- **Fold:** Standing fold (11" x 24" x 20.25"). Self-folds at the push of a button on the handle — genuinely one-hand, one-step operation. Stands upright when folded, which is convenient for entryways and bus rides
- **Seat:** Multi-position recline. Suitable for infants to toddlers up to 50 lbs. *Not newborn-flat without car seat*
- **Canopy:** UPF 50+, water-repellent. Good coverage
- **Basket:** Moderate size; easier access than YOYO
- **Terrain:** Smooth ride on city surfaces; adequate suspension
- **Car seat compatibility:** All Nuna PIPA series seats click in directly — no adapter needed. This is a real advantage if you go with a Nuna car seat. Limited third-party compatibility
- **One-hand fold:** Yes — true one-hand self-fold. Best fold mechanism in the group
- **Price:** ~$350-$450 depending on color. TRVL lx (premium version) ~$450-$575
- **Includes:** Carry bag included
- **Best for:** Parents who want the easiest possible fold and plan to use a Nuna car seat
- **Weakness:** Best experience is locked to Nuna ecosystem; standing fold dimensions are taller than the flat-folding YOYO

(Source: [Nuna Official](https://nunababy.com/usa/trvl-easy-fold-compact-stroller), [BabyGearLab TRVL Review](https://www.babygearlab.com/reviews/getting-around/travel-stroller/nuna-trvl))

#### Bugaboo Butterfly 2 — Best Canopy & Storage

- **Weight:** 16.1 lbs — heavier than the ultra-light options
- **Fold:** Compact (17.6" x 9.6" x 21.8"). IATA-compliant. One-second fold advertised. Stands when folded
- **Seat:** Fully flat recline — usable from birth without accessories. Suitable up to ~50 lbs. One-hand adjustable footrest
- **Canopy:** Best-in-class. Large, extendable UPF 50+ canopy with peekaboo window. Detachable. Actually provides full sun coverage — matters for a July baby
- **Basket:** Largest basket in this class. Fits oversized diaper bags. Rear-access
- **Terrain:** Larger wheels than original Butterfly. All-wheel suspension. Handles city terrain including rougher pavement well
- **Car seat compatibility:** Nuna PIPA, Maxi-Cosi (Coral XP, Mico series), Cybex (Cloud, Aton), Clek Liing — all via adapter (sold separately, ~$40)
- **One-hand fold:** Close but not perfectly one-handed. Very fast fold, but some reviewers note it's a two-hand initiation
- **Price:** ~$599 — most expensive in this group
- **Best for:** Parents who prioritize canopy coverage (summer baby!), storage basket, and from-birth usability
- **Weakness:** Heaviest option, most expensive, brake system can be stiff

(Source: [Bugaboo Official](https://www.bugaboo.com/us-en/strollers/bugaboo-butterfly-2/), [Tom's Guide Butterfly 2 Review](https://www.tomsguide.com/home/bugaboo-butterfly-2-review), [The Stroller Mom](https://thestrollermomblog.com/stroller/bugaboo-butterfly-2-honest-review/))

#### UPPAbaby MINU V3 — Premium Build Quality

- **Weight:** 16.7 lbs — heaviest in this group
- **Fold:** Compact standing fold (10" x 18" x 21.5"). IATA-compliant. One-hand, one-step fold AND unfold — improved over V2
- **Seat:** Fully flat, newborn-friendly recline with built-in infant barrier. From birth to 50 lbs / 40" tall. GREENGUARD Gold certified (low chemical emissions)
- **Canopy:** Large, excellent coverage. UPPAbaby canopies are consistently among the best. Peek-a-boo window
- **Basket:** 20 lb capacity, generous size. Best basket-to-stroller-size ratio
- **Terrain:** All-wheel suspension. Handles uneven sidewalks and gravel well — one of the best performers on rough terrain in the compact class
- **Car seat compatibility:** UPPAbaby Mesa/Aria (direct), Maxi-Cosi, Nuna, Cybex, Clek via adapter kits (~$20-40). Broadest compatibility in this group
- **One-hand fold:** Yes — true one-hand fold and unfold. Improved mechanism over V2
- **Price:** ~$500. Includes travel bag
- **Leather handlebar:** Standard on all colors
- **Best for:** Parents who want premium materials, best ride quality, and broadest car seat compatibility in a compact package
- **Weakness:** Heaviest option by 3+ lbs over the lightest; that matters on subway stairs. No bassinet compatibility (V2 had this)

(Source: [UPPAbaby Official](https://uppababy.com/strollers/lightweight/minu-v3/), [Fathercraft](https://fathercraft.com/best-travel-strollers/), [Babesta MINU V2 vs V3](https://babesta.com/blogs/news/minu-v2-vs-v3-all-you-need-to-know))

### Travel System Possibilities

If you want a matched car seat + stroller combo:

- **Nuna PIPA + Nuna TRVL** — Seamless click-in, no adapter needed. PIPA urbn (~$350) + TRVL (~$350) = ~$700 total. Nuna sells this as an official travel system
- **UPPAbaby Mesa V2 + MINU V3** — Direct compatibility. Mesa V2 (~$350) + MINU V3 (~$500) = ~$850 total
- **Cybex Cloud G + Libelle 2** — Cybex ecosystem. Cloud G (~$250-350) + Libelle 2 (~$300) = ~$550-650 total. Most budget-friendly combo
- **Mix and match** — Most of these strollers work with Nuna, Maxi-Cosi, and Cybex seats via adapters. Decide car seat first, then pick the stroller with the best adapter support

> **Note:** Car seat decision should come first — see [car seat](car-seat.md). Then match the stroller. All five strollers above work with at least 2-3 major car seat brands.

### Summer Baby Considerations

Since baby is due July 2026:
- **Canopy coverage matters.** The Bugaboo Butterfly 2 and UPPAbaby MINU V3 have the best sun protection
- **Ventilation matters.** The Stokke YOYO3 has a perforated seat for airflow. The MINU V3 has mesh back panel
- **UPF 50+ canopies** are standard on all five models
- Heat can be brutal in a stroller with poor airflow — prioritize canopy + ventilation if you'll be out in July/August
