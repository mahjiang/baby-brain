## Philosophy

A registry isn't just a gift list — it's a research tool, price tracker, and completion-discount strategy. The goal: get the best stuff at the best prices with minimal waste.

**Key insight:** Add everything you might want to your registry, including items you plan to buy yourself. After the shower, whatever wasn't gifted becomes eligible for 15-20% off via completion discounts. The registry is a savings tool, not just a wishlist.

> **Apartment tip:** Avoid registering for bulky items that need storage before baby arrives. A diaper fund is better than a diaper stockpile when you have limited closet space. Same for clothes — register for future sizes (6-12mo) so you're not drowning in stuff now.

### Skip / Wait

- **Shoes** — They don't walk for ~12 months
- **Baby food maker** — A regular blender works
- **Wipe warmer** — Nice-to-have at best; wipes at room temp are fine
- **Too many newborn-size clothes** — They outgrow them in days-weeks; you'll get hand-me-downs
- **Baby bath tub** — A sink or regular tub works for many families; wait and see
- **Bottle warmer** — A cup of warm water works
- **Matching crib bedding set** — Bumpers, blankets, and pillows are **unsafe** per AAP. All you need is a fitted sheet on a firm mattress
