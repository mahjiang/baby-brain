## Product Research

### Swaddles & Sleep Sacks

**What to know:**
- Swaddling improves sleep by dampening the Moro (startle) reflex. *Evidence:* Research shows swaddled infants are less likely to transition from startling to wakefulness. (Source: [ParentData — Emily Oster](https://parentdata.org/qa-dangerous-swaddle-baby/))
- **Stop swaddling when baby shows signs of rolling** — typically 2-4 months. A swaddled baby who rolls face-down cannot use arms to reposition. (Source: [AAP Safe Sleep 2022](https://publications.aap.org/pediatrics/article/150/1/e2022057990/188304))
- **No weighted swaddles or sleep sacks** — AAP recommends against; CPSC investigated fatalities; major retailers pulled weighted sleepwear April 2024. Skip Nested Bean Zen and Dreamland Baby. (Source: [CPSC Commissioner Statement](https://www.cpsc.gov/About-CPSC/Commissioner/Richard-Trumka/Statement/Beware-Weighted-Infant-Swaddles-and-Blankets-Are-Unsafe-for-Sleep-Retailers-Should-Consider-Stopping-Sales))
- **TOG ratings for July baby:** 0.2 TOG for 77°F+, 0.5 TOG for 74-78°F (most summer nights with AC), 1.0 TOG for 69-73°F

### Baby Monitor (Video)

**WiFi vs. Non-WiFi — the key decision:**

| | WiFi (Smart) Monitors | Non-WiFi (Dedicated) Monitors |
|--|----------------------|------------------------------|
| **How it works** | Streams over your home WiFi to a phone app | Dedicated encrypted radio signal to a parent unit |
| **Pros** | View from anywhere (work, travel); often higher resolution; smart features (sleep tracking, AI alerts) | **No hacking risk** (no internet connection); no app/subscription; more reliable (no WiFi drops); simpler setup |
| **Cons** | Hackable if poorly secured; depends on WiFi reliability; may require subscription for features; latency/lag possible | Can only view on parent unit (limited range); lower resolution than top WiFi cameras; no remote access |
| **For apartment** | Overkill — you're always within 50 feet of baby | **Ideal** — short range is not a limitation; reliability matters most |

> **Our situation:** In an apartment, you are never far from baby. A non-WiFi monitor eliminates hacking concerns entirely and is more reliable (no WiFi drops at 3 AM). The range limitation of non-WiFi monitors (typically 800-1000 ft) is irrelevant in an apartment.

### Pacifiers

**Evidence:** The AAP recommends offering a pacifier at nap time and bedtime as a SIDS risk reduction strategy — this is an **A-level recommendation** (highest evidence grade). A meta-analysis of 7 studies found pacifier use reduces SIDS risk with an adjusted OR of 0.39 (roughly 61% relative risk reduction). In absolute terms, the NNT is ~2,733. Since pacifiers cost ~$3 and have minimal downside, it's an easy "yes." (Source: [AAP 2022 Safe Sleep](https://publications.aap.org/pediatrics/article/150/1/e2022057990/188304), [Hauck et al., Pediatrics 2005](https://publications.aap.org/pediatrics/article/116/5/e716/68083))

**When to introduce:** For breastfed infants, delay until breastfeeding is established (~3-4 weeks). Formula-fed can start anytime.

**What to look for:** One-piece construction (can't separate → no choking hazard), silicone over latex (more durable, hypoallergenic, dishwasher-safe), age-appropriate sizing.

| Brand | Price | Construction | Shape | Situation Fit |
|-------|-------|-------------|-------|---------------|
| **Philips Avent Soothie** | ~$4-6 / 2-pack | One-piece silicone | Round | **Best starter** — #1 hospital pacifier in the US. Cheapest, safest construction. |
| **Dr. Brown's HappyPaci** | ~$6-8 / 3-pack | One-piece silicone | Orthodontic | **Best value** — one-piece, orthodontic shape, open-back prevents mold |
| **MAM Original** | ~$7-9 / 2-pack | Multi-piece | Orthodontic (thin, flat) | Good — popular with babies, but multi-piece (mold risk in nipple) |

> **Our pick:** Buy one pack each of **Philips Avent Soothie** + **Dr. Brown's HappyPaci** (~$12 total). Don't stock up — babies are picky and may reject your first tries. Not worth registry space at this price point.

(Sources: [AAP 2022 Safe Sleep](https://publications.aap.org/pediatrics/article/150/1/e2022057990/188304), [Consumer Reports Pacifier Testing 2025](https://www.consumerreports.org/babies-kids/pacifiers/best-pacifiers-a1146491761/), [AAPD Pacifier Policy](https://www.aapd.org/globalassets/media/policies_guidelines/p_pacifiers.pdf))

### Baby Laundry Detergent

**All Free Clear** — ~$12-14 for 88 oz (58 loads)

"Baby detergent" is largely marketing. Pediatric dermatologists say the key is avoiding **fragrances** (#1 contact allergen), **dyes**, and **optical brighteners**. Any detergent free of all three is fine for newborn skin. **Dreft scores an F on the EWG scale** due to sodium borate, fragrance, and optical brighteners — the very ingredients to avoid. All Free Clear is the #1 dermatologist-recommended detergent for sensitive skin. Use for the whole household — no need to wash baby clothes separately.

(Sources: [NBC News — Dermatologist-Recommended Baby Detergents](https://www.nbcnews.com/select/shopping/best-baby-detergent-rcna187408), [BabyGearLab — Best Baby Detergent](https://www.babygearlab.com/topics/clothing-shoes/best-baby-detergent))

### Thermometer

**Exergen Smart Glow Temporal Artery Thermometer** — ~$34

**Status:** Decided

Temporal (forehead swipe) thermometers are the most accurate non-rectal method per AAP and can be used from birth. The Exergen is the clinical standard — used in most US hospitals, backed by 100+ published clinical studies, takes 1,000 readings per second and selects the most accurate. Swipe across forehead from temple to temple. Reads ~0.5-1°F lower than rectal — your pediatrician will account for this.

> **Critical:** A temp of 100.4°F (38°C) or higher (rectal equivalent) in an infant under 3 months is a medical emergency. Call your pediatrician immediately or go to the ER. For temporal readings, ask your pediatrician what threshold they want you to call at.

(Sources: [AAP — How to Take Your Child's Temperature](https://www.healthychildren.org/English/health-issues/conditions/fever/Pages/How-to-Take-a-Childs-Temperature.aspx), [AAP News — Temporal artery thermometers may rival rectal](https://publications.aap.org/aapnews/article/34/4/2/10563/Temporal-artery-thermometers-may-rival-rectal), [Exergen TAT-2000C](https://www.exergen.com/product/tat-2000c-temporal-artery-thermometer/))

**Skip buying ahead:** Infant ibuprofen (not until 6 months), gripe water (no evidence), teething gels (FDA warned against benzocaine), any medication without pediatrician approval.

(Sources: [AAP — Acetaminophen Dosing](https://www.healthychildren.org/English/safety-prevention/at-home/medication-safety/Pages/Acetaminophen-for-Fever-and-Pain.aspx), [Children's Hospital Colorado — Baby First-Aid Kit](https://www.childrenscolorado.org/just-ask-childrens/articles/baby-first-aid-kit/))

## What You Do NOT Need

- Wipe warmer
- Bottle warmer (a cup of warm water works)
- Baby shoes (until they walk)
- Matching crib bedding sets (bumpers, blankets, pillows — these are **unsafe**, per AAP)
- Stuffed animals in the crib (**unsafe** for sleep)
- Baby food processor (regular blender/fork works)
- Too many newborn-size clothes (they outgrow fast)
