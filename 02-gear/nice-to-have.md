# Nice-to-Have Items

> Items that are helpful but not essential. Good registry additions. Don't buy until you see what you get as gifts and what baby actually likes.

## First 3 Months

| Item | Why | Priority | Notes |
|------|-----|----------|-------|
| Bouncer / rocker | Somewhere safe to set baby down | Medium | Baby Bjorn bouncer is popular; some babies hate them |
| Swing | Soothes some babies | Low | Takes up space; try bouncer first |
| Play mat / activity gym | Tummy time, stimulation | Medium | Useful from ~1 month; Lovevery is popular but pricey |
| Portable sound machine | For outings, naps on the go | Medium | Yogasleep Hushh or similar |
| Diaper bag / backpack | Organized outings | Medium | A regular backpack works fine too |
| Baby bathtub / bath flower | Easier than sink | Low | Skip if sink works for you |
| Night light (dimmable) | Night feeds without waking everyone | Medium | Hatch Rest does double duty as sound machine |

## 3–6 Months

| Item | Why | Notes |
|------|-----|-------|
| Exersaucer / jumper | Contained entertainment once baby can hold head up | Big footprint — skip if space is tight |
| High chair | Starting solids ~6 months | See [high-chair.md](high-chair.md) — Abiie Beyond recommended, IKEA Antilop as backup |
| Teething toys | Some babies teethe early | |
| Board books | Start reading early | Great gift/registry item |

## Nice but Not Necessary

- Baby Brezza (formula machine) — convenient but expensive
- Owlet or similar monitor sock — AAP does not recommend consumer oxygen monitors for healthy infants
- Fancy nursery decor — baby doesn't care
- Baby shoes — not until walking

### Bouncer / Rocker

| Product | Price | Pros | Cons | Verdict |
|---------|-------|------|------|---------|
| **BabyBjorn Bouncer Bliss** | ~$230 | No batteries/plugs — baby's own movement rocks it. Only 5 lbs, super portable. Folds flat. Machine-washable cover. Lasts through toddlerhood. | Expensive. No vibration or toy bar (sold separately). Some babies prefer powered motion. | **Best if budget allows.** Apartment-friendly (compact, light, folds flat). High resale value. |

**Our situation:** In an apartment, the BabyBjorn's portability and compact fold are real advantages. But if you are not sure baby will even like a bouncer, the Fisher-Price is low-risk at $40-60. Consider putting the BabyBjorn on the registry and buying the Fisher-Price if nobody gifts it.

### Nursing Chair / Glider

*Added 2026-03-01*

A nursing chair is **not essential** — your couch, bed, or any comfortable chair with a nursing pillow works. But with 8-12+ feeds per day in the early months, a dedicated chair with good arm support and the ability to rock/glide makes a meaningful comfort difference, especially for night feeds and rocking baby to sleep.

| Product | Price | Key Features | Footprint | Verdict |
|---------|-------|-------------|-----------|---------|
| **DaVinci Suzy Recliner & Swivel Glider** | ~$300-350 | Swivels, reclines, metal base, easy assembly | ~27" wide | **Best budget pick.** Compact enough for apartments, does everything you need. |
| **Babyletto Toco Swivel Glider** | ~$350-400 | 360° swivel, high back, Greenguard Gold certified, eco-friendly fabrics | ~25" wide | **Best compact option.** Smallest footprint of the bunch; good for tight spaces. |
| **Nurture& The Glider Plus** | ~$750-850 | Power recline, built-in USB charger, lumbar support, water-resistant fabric | ~30" wide | **Registry dream pick.** Premium features but expensive and bigger. |
| **Skip the chair** | ~$50 total | Existing couch or bed + nursing pillow + $15 footstool | 0" added | **Lowest cost, smallest footprint.** Covers ~80% of the benefit. |

**Our situation:** In an apartment with no nursery, a full-size glider (30"+) is a hard fit. Compact options (25-27" wide) are more realistic. This is a strong registry candidate — it's a bigger-ticket item people like to gift, and if nobody buys it, you fall back to existing furniture + a nursing pillow with zero downside. If buying yourself, the DaVinci Suzy at ~$300 is the sweet spot for moderate budget.

(Sources: [BabyGearLab — Best Nursery Gliders](https://www.babygearlab.com/topics/nursery/best-nursery-glider), [Babylist — Best Nursery Chairs & Gliders](https://www.babylist.com/hello-baby/best-nursery-gliders-rockers))
