# Recommended Reading

## Pregnancy & Birth

| Book | Author | Focus | Mini Review |
|------|--------|-------|-------------|
| *Expecting Better* | Emily Oster | Evidence-based pregnancy decisions | Data-driven look at what actually matters. Great for the researcher type. |
| *The Birth Partner* | Penny Simkin | Labor support for partners | The go-to guide for the non-birthing partner. Practical and reassuring. |
| *Ina May's Guide to Childbirth* | Ina May Gaskin | Natural childbirth | Empowering birth stories. More philosophy than data. |

## Newborn & Parenting

| Book | Author | Focus | Mini Review |
|------|--------|-------|-------------|
| *Cribsheet* | Emily Oster | Evidence-based parenting (0-toddler) | Sequel to Expecting Better. Covers breastfeeding, sleep, childcare with data. **Read this before the baby arrives.** |
| *The Family Firm* | Emily Oster | Evidence-based decisions (toddler+) | Continues Oster's series; covers return to work, preschool, discipline. Less urgent now but relevant later. |
| *Happiest Baby on the Block* | Harvey Karp MD | Soothing newborns (5 S's) | Practical techniques for calming a fussy baby. Widely used; effective. |
| *Precious Little Sleep* | Alexis Dubief | Baby sleep | Practical, evidence-informed sleep guide. Best general sleep book for the first year. |
| *It's Never Too Late to Sleep Train* | Craig Canapari MD | Sleep training | Yale pediatric sleep specialist. More clinical than Dubief; excellent for specific situations and troubleshooting. |
| *Brain Rules for Baby* | John Medina | Child development (0-5) | Neuroscience of early childhood. Accessible and well-cited. |
| *The Gardener and the Carpenter* | Alison Gopnik | Child development science | UC Berkeley developmental psychologist. More rigorous than popular books; challenges the "optimize your child" mindset with actual developmental science. Less practical, more foundational. |
| *Nurture Shock* | Po Bronson & Ashley Merryman | Counterintuitive parenting research | Journalistic synthesis of developmental research surprises (sleep, race, praise). Good; verify specific citations independently. |
