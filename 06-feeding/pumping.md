# Breast Pump & Pumping

> **Check insurance by:** Week 24-28 | Most insurance plans cover a breast pump at no cost under the ACA.

## Insurance Coverage

- [ ] Call insurance to ask about breast pump benefit
- [ ] Questions to ask:
  - Which pumps are covered?
  - Can I choose any brand or from a specific list?
  - Do I need a prescription from my OB?
  - When can I order? (Some allow it at 30 weeks)
  - Through which vendor? (Aeroflow, Edgepark, etc.)

## Pump Types

| Type | Pros | Cons | Examples |
|------|------|------|---------|
| **Double electric** | Fast, efficient, standard | Plugged in or battery, less discreet | Spectra S1/S2, Medela Pump In Style |
| **Wearable / portable** | Hands-free, discreet, no cords | Pricier, smaller capacity, some leak | Willow, Elvie, Momcozy |
| **Manual** | Cheap, portable, quiet | Slow, hand fatigue | Medela Harmony, Haakaa (passive) |
| **Hospital-grade** (rental) | Most powerful, for supply issues | Expensive to rent, not portable | Medela Symphony |

## What Else You Need for Pumping

- [ ] Breast milk storage bags
- [ ] Bottles compatible with pump
- [ ] Extra pump parts (flanges, valves, membranes)
- [ ] Correct flange size (most pumps come with 24mm; many people need different)
- [ ] Cooler bag for transport/storage
- [ ] Bottle brush and drying rack
