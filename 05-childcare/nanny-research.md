# Nanny Research

> See [decision framework](decision-framework.md) for how nannies compare to other options.

## Cost Estimates

| Type | Hourly Range | Monthly (Full-Time) | Notes |
|------|-------------|---------------------|-------|
| Full-time nanny | $18–$30+/hr | $2,500–$5,000+ | Varies significantly by city |
| Part-time nanny | $18–$30+/hr | Pro-rated | |
| Nanny share | Split cost with another family | ~50-70% of full nanny cost | |

*Don't forget: payroll taxes, PTO, sick days, overtime. You are the employer.*

## Finding a Nanny

- [ ] Agencies (vetted but pricier — placement fee)
- [ ] Care.com, UrbanSitter, Sittercity
- [ ] Local parent groups / recommendations
- [ ] Neighborhood apps (Nextdoor, Facebook groups)

## Interview Questions

- [ ] Experience with infants? CPR/First Aid certified?
- [ ] What does a typical day look like with a baby?
- [ ] How do you handle a crying baby that won't settle?
- [ ] Comfort with cats?
- [ ] Driving? (if applicable)
- [ ] What are your expectations for PTO, holidays, sick days?
- [ ] References? Background check?
- [ ] Legal to work in the US?

## Legal / Tax Considerations

- Nannies are W-2 employees (not 1099 contractors)
- You must pay employer portion of Social Security and Medicare
- Look into nanny payroll services (HomePay, SurePayroll, Poppins Payroll)
- May need workers' comp insurance (varies by state)
- Dependent Care FSA can help offset costs (up to $5,000/year pre-tax)
