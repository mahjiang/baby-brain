# Childcare Decision Framework

> **Status:** Researching | **Priority:** HIGH — Start NOW. Urban waitlists can be 6-12+ months.

## Why This Is Urgent at Week 20

Many urban daycares have waitlists of 6-12+ months. Even if you haven't decided between daycare and a nanny, **get on daycare waitlists now.** You can always decline a spot later. You can't get time back.

- [ ] Research local daycare options — see [daycare research](daycare-research.md)
- [ ] Get on at least 2-3 waitlists this month
- [ ] Discuss daycare vs nanny high-level preference with partner

---

## Options Overview

| Option | Monthly Cost (US Urban, estimate) | Ages | Pros | Cons |
|--------|----------------------------------|------|------|------|
| **Daycare center** | $1,500–$3,000+ | 6 wks–5 yrs | Structured, socialization, regulated, reliable | Waitlists, germs, less flexible hours, closures |
| **Home daycare** | $1,000–$2,200 | Varies | Smaller groups, often cheaper, more personal | Less regulated (varies by state), single-provider risk |
| **Nanny (full-time)** | $2,500–$5,000+ | Any | 1-on-1 care, flexible, in your home, sick-day coverage | Most expensive, HR complexity (taxes, backup), isolation |
| **Nanny share** | $1,500–$3,000 | Any | Split nanny cost, some socialization | Coordination with other family, schedule alignment |
| **Family member** | $0–varies | Any | Trusted, flexible, free/cheap | Boundary challenges, may not be sustainable long-term |
| **Au pair** | $1,500–$2,000 (all-in) | Any | Cultural exchange, flexible, includes light housework | Need spare room (may not work for apartment), 1-year commitment |

*Costs are rough estimates for US urban/suburban areas. Actual costs vary significantly by location.*

## Our Situation Factors

Consider these when evaluating options:

| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Apartment (no spare room)** | Au pair likely doesn't work; nanny comes to your space |
| **Two working parents with paid leave** | Stagger leave? Need care starting month ___ |
| **Car + transit** | Daycare doesn't need to be walkable but proximity helps |
| **Cat** | Nanny in your home = cat is fine; daycare = no cat factor |
| **Budget: moderate** | Nanny may stretch budget; daycare or nanny share more feasible |
| **Complications** | If baby arrives early, care timeline may shift |

### Key Questions to Decide Together

> Discuss these with your partner:
>
> 1. When does the first parent go back to work? When does the second?
> 2. What's our maximum monthly childcare budget?
> 3. Do we have family nearby who could help regularly?
> 4. How important is socialization with other babies vs 1-on-1 attention?
> 5. How do we feel about a stranger in our home daily (nanny) vs dropping off (daycare)?
